Ingredients:

  • 1 lb chicken breast fillets (cut into strips)
  • ½ cup flour (for dredging)
  • 2 large eggs (beaten)
  • 1 cup breadcrumbs (panko for extra crunch)
  • ½ tsp garlic powder
  • ½ tsp paprika
  • Salt and pepper(optional)
  • Oil for frying(preferably canola oil)

Instructions:

    Getting the Chicken Ready:

  1. Start by cutting your chicken breast fillets into nice, even strips. Once cut, give them a gentle pat with paper towels to soak up any extra moisture.

    2. Setting up your Dredging Stations

  2. Grab three shallow dishes. In the first one, pour some flour and sprinkle in a pinch of salt and pepper if you like. - In the second dish, crack two eggs and beat them until well mixed. - For the third dish, combine breadcrumbs with garlic powder and paprika for that extra flavor kick.

    3. Dredge em Chicken Tendies

  3. - Take a chicken strip and first coat it in the flour, shaking off any excess. - Next, dip it into the beaten eggs, making sure every bit is covered. - Finally, roll the strip in the breadcrumb mixture, pressing down gently.

    4. Heating The Oil

  4. - In a large skillet, pour in about half an inch of canola oil and heat it over medium-high. You’ll know it’s ready when you drop a breadcrumb in and it sizzles right away.

    5. Fry the Chicken

  5. - Carefully add the breaded chicken strips to the hot oil in batches, making sure not to overcrowd the pan. This helps them cook evenly. - Fry each side for about 4-5 minutes, or until they turn a beautiful golden brown and are cooked through (make sure the internal temperature hits 165°F).

    6. Drain the Excess Oil

  6. - Once they’re golden and crispy, use tongs to take the chicken strips out of the oil and set them on a plate lined with paper towels. This will help soak up any extra oil.

    7. Cooked and Ready!

  7. - Serve those crispy chicken strips warm, and don’t forget to add your favorite dipping sauces like ketchup, barbecue, or honey mustard.
